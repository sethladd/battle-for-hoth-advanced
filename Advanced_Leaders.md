# Battle of Hoth — Advanced Leaders (on-board characters)

In the basic game a leader is just three command cards shuffled into your deck. The
**Advanced Leader** rules put the character on the battlefield as a miniature that rides
with a unit, strengthens it, inspires the troops around it, and can be hunted down. Each
leader still brings their three command cards; now they also bring a body on the board.

## The general system

**Attachment.** A leader is a single character figure that shares a hex with one friendly
unit — its **escort**. The hex is still a single unit for stacking and targeting; the
leader figure simply rides along. At setup you place your leader with any *eligible* unit
(the eligible unit type is listed per leader below).

**Movement.** When the escort unit is ordered and moves, the leader moves with it. A leader
may also **transfer**: when you order the escort, instead of moving it you may move the
leader figure alone up to 1 hex to join another friendly unit. A leader can never stand on
a hex by itself (except as noted for Vader) — it is always with a unit.

**Combat.** The escort attacks normally and adds the leader's combat bonus (extra dice,
rerolls, etc.). The leader never rolls a separate attack; it empowers its escort.

**Aura.** While the leader figure is in play, its aura applies to nearby friendly units
(range given per leader). Auras do not stack with themselves.

**Command cards.** The leader's three cards stay in the deck and are played normally. Most
now read "the unit with [Leader]…" so they hit harder when the character is in the fight.

**Leaders are targets — Bonus Medals.** When a leader's escort unit is eliminated, the
leader tries to escape:
- If a friendly unit is in an adjacent hex, the leader immediately joins it (survives, no
  medal awarded for the leader).
- If no friendly unit is adjacent, the leader is **captured or killed**: the opponent gains
  the leader's **Bonus Medal** value (below), in addition to the medal for the unit.

This is the core tension: a leader makes a unit dangerous, but a leader caught alone and
isolated is a windfall for the enemy. Players must screen their commander with supporting
units.

**Off-board option.** A leader may instead be run *off-board* (fleet/HQ). An off-board
leader has no figure, no aura, and cannot be eliminated — but also adds no board presence.
**Admiral Piett is always off-board**; the others may optionally be played off-board for a
weaker, safer game (they then function as in the basic rules).

## Rebel Alliance leaders

### Luke Skywalker — "Rogue Leader" (pilots a Snowspeeder)
- **Mini / escort:** rides a **Snowspeeder** unit (vehicle, move 0–3, attack 4/2).
- **In the cockpit:** the Luke-speeder rolls **+1 attack die**, may **reroll one** of its
  attack dice each attack (The Force), and **ignores 1 Retreat** rolled against it.
- **Aura (1 hex):** adjacent friendly units may ignore 1 Retreat forced on them.
- **Cards:** Force Push, Trust Your Feelings, Heroic Resolve.
- **Bonus Medal: 2.** If his speeder is destroyed and no friendly unit is adjacent, Luke is
  lost (Empire +2). Otherwise he ejects to an adjacent unit and fights on as a foot Jedi
  escort (escort attacks +1 die, close combat only).

### Han Solo — on a **Tauntaun** (scout cavalry)
- **Mini / escort:** a new single-figure **Tauntaun rider** unit. Move 0–4 (fast), may
  ignore the first terrain "must stop" each turn, attack **2 dice, close combat only**.
  *(No tauntaun mini in the box — proxy with any rider figure.)*
- **Scoundrel:** after attacking, Han may **move 1–2 hexes** (hit-and-run), and may
  **reroll one die** once per turn (Scoundrel's Luck).
- **Aura (1 hex):** adjacent friendly units gain **+1 movement**.
- **Cards:** Never Tell Me the Odds, Surprise Attack, Covering Fire.
- **Bonus Medal: 1.** If the tauntaun is killed, Han joins an adjacent unit on foot; if
  isolated, Empire +1.

### Leia Organa — with a **command squad**
- **Mini / escort:** joins an **Echo Base trooper** unit, adding a **4th figure** (a
  command squad of 4 instead of 3).
- **Command:** the squad with Leia rolls **+1 attack die**; if it does not attack, it may
  **return 1 lost figure** (rally), up to its 4-figure cap.
- **Aura (2 hexes):** friendly units within 2 hexes roll +1 die when attacking
  (Command Network). *(This replaces Leia's basic "first ordered unit +1 die" passive.)*
- **Cards:** Coordinated Defense, New Hope, Deploy the Fleet.
- **Bonus Medal: 2.** A high-value capture target; screen her well.

## Galactic Empire leaders

### Darth Vader — Sith Lord (on foot, with the troops)
- **Mini / escort:** joins any **Snowtrooper** unit.
- **Power of the Dark Side:** the unit with Vader rolls **+2 dice in close combat**;
  Vader's attacks treat **Retreat results as hits** and **ignore the target's terrain
  protection** in close combat.
- **Aura — Fear (1 hex):** enemy units adjacent to Vader roll **1 fewer die** when
  attacking, and any retreat they are forced to make near him is **+1 hex**.
- **Cards:** Force Choke, Lack of Faith, Power of the Dark Side.
- **Survival:** Vader is hard to remove. If his escort is eliminated he **always survives**
  and joins any adjacent friendly unit; if none is adjacent he becomes a **lone Sith
  figure** (a 1-figure "unit," close combat 3 dice) that fights on. Only by eliminating
  *that* lone figure does the Rebel player score his **Bonus Medal: 2.**

### General Veers — in an **AT-AT** ("Blizzard 1")
- **Mini / escort:** commands an **AT-AT** unit (keeps the AT-AT's confirmation/ignore-
  retreat/no-terrain-protection rules).
- **Armored Spearhead:** the Veers walker rolls **+1 attack die**; all friendly **vehicle**
  units roll +1 die in close combat.
- **Aura (2 hexes):** friendly units within 2 hexes of the walker may **ignore 1 terrain
  movement restriction** (advancing in its shadow).
- **Cards:** Concentrate All Fire, Maximum Firepower, Break Their Lines.
- **Bonus Medal: 1.** If the AT-AT is destroyed (its confirmation roll), Veers goes down
  with it — Rebel +1 in addition to the AT-AT medal.

### Admiral Piett — **off-board** (Fleet Command)
- **No mini.** Represents orbital coordination from the fleet.
- **Fleet Command:** you hold **+1 command card**; once per game you may call down his
  bombardment (his card).
- **Aura:** none (off-board).
- **Cards:** Orbital Bombardment, Tactical Redeployment, Special Orders.
- **Bonus Medal: 0** — cannot be eliminated. Piett trades all board presence for total
  safety, the deliberate counterpoint to the front-line leaders.

## Setup, scenarios, and balance

- **Setup:** after deploying forces, each player places their leader figure with an
  eligible friendly unit (Piett stays off-board). The map annotator can mark the leader's
  starting unit so the simulator places it.
- **Victory:** Bonus Medals from killing a leader count toward the scenario's victory
  total, so a scenario that wins at 4 medals can be decided by a single bold decapitation
  strike — exciting, but swingy, which is why the values are 1–2 and escape is usually
  possible.
- **Balance levers (for the simulator's tuning pass):** Bonus-Medal values (1 vs 2), aura
  range (1 vs 2 hexes), whether transfer costs the escort's order, and whether off-board
  play is allowed. All are isolated so they can be tuned without touching the deck.

These rules keep everything inside the existing system — same hexes, same dice, same
command cards, same medals — while making each leader a character you maneuver, protect,
and hunt.
