# Battle of Hoth — Advanced Units

Two new units round out the advanced game, each filling a gap between the existing light
and heavy units. Stats are tuned against the base Hoth units and borrow from the
Richard Borg lineage (Memoir '44 / Commands & Colors), where cavalry and medium armor
occupy exactly these roles.

## Tauntaun Scout — Rebel light cavalry

Fast-moving mounted scouts who harry the Imperial flanks and vanish into the snow.

- **Figures:** 2 (mounted riders)
- **Type:** infantry for hit purposes (hit on an **infantry** symbol or a **blast**)
- **Move:** 0–3 hexes; may still attack after moving up to 2 (a mounted charge)
- **Attack:** **3 dice in close combat**, **1 die at range 2** (a melee skirmisher)
- **All-terrain:** ignores the first "must stop" from rocks/wreckage each turn (sure-footed)
- **Hit-and-run:** after resolving an attack it may **move 1 hex** (take ground / disengage)
- **Eliminating a Tauntaun Scout grants a victory medal.**

*Design / inspiration:* this is the Borg-system **cavalry** template — high movement, a
strong close-combat punch that falls off at range, and the signature "battle, then move"
ability (Memoir '44 armor overrun / C&C cavalry momentum). It gives the Rebels a
proactive, mobile threat to pair with the defensive troopers and the fragile Snowspeeders,
and it's the natural mount for **Han Solo** in the Advanced Leader rules.

## AT-ST — Imperial scout walker (medium armor)

The two-legged "chicken walker" — nimbler than an AT-AT, far tougher than a Snowspeeder.

- **Figures:** 2 (a single walker that can soak two solid hits)
- **Type:** vehicle (hit on a **vehicle** symbol or a **blast**)
- **Move:** 0–2 hexes; may still attack after moving (mobile gun platform)
- **Attack:** **3 / 3 / 2 dice** at range 1 / 2 / 3 (a medium tank's firepower)
- **Nimble:** unlike the AT-AT it **may climb onto ridge hexes**; it cannot cross
  crevasses or seracs
- **Vulnerable to suppression:** it has **no AT-AT confirmation rule** and **does not
  ignore retreats** — it dies to 2 vehicle/blast hits and can be pushed back like any unit;
  it also receives normal terrain protection
- **Eliminating an AT-ST grants a victory medal.**

*Design / inspiration:* modeled on the **Memoir '44 tank** (3/3/3, mobile, takes ground)
but trimmed to 3/3/2 with 2 figures and move 2 so it sits cleanly **between the Snowspeeder
and the AT-AT** rather than rivaling the heavy walker. It gives the Empire a maneuver
element that can take ground and contest objectives without committing an irreplaceable
AT-AT, and it's the lighter companion to **General Veers'** command walker.
